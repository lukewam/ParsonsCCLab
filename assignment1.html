ASSIGNMENT 01

LOST AND FOUND

A01

Project Reflection

My partner described looking out of a window with a grid and circular patterns. Outside, there were pine trees in front of a beach. The view was from the seventh floor, and a bird was flying in the distance.

I interpreted this as a view of the ocean, framed by pine trees on each side. At first, I imagined the window covered with many small circular patterns and divided into four panels. Visually, though, that version felt too busy. The composition was complex in a way that didn’t read clearly as an image.

As I kept working, I simplified the scene. I reduced the circles to a few large ones at the center, and streamlined the window structure so it felt more like a strong geometric frame rather than a distraction. The goal was to keep the idea of the patterned glass without overwhelming the view behind it.

In the final composition, four rectangles along the edges define the window frame, with four large circles in the center as the window pattern. The pine trees are built from stacked triangles and a single rectangle for the trunk. The bird was the hardest element to abstract; I eventually constructed it from two circles for the body and three triangles for the wings and tail.
